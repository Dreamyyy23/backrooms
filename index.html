The yellow is wrong. It's not just a color; it's a sickness. It smells of old wet wool, decaying wallpaper, and stagnant moisture that gets trapped behind your teeth.

The fluorescent hum is so loud it vibrates through your jawbone. There are no doors, no windows, only the infinite repetition of randomly segmented empty offices. Six hundred million square miles of silent isolation.

If you stop moving, the silence between the light hums starts whispering things you forgot in the hospital. If you hear something crawling nearby... it has already been hunting you for miles.

THE FOXHOLE BLEEDS THROUGH

Someone has stapled a tiny orange thread to the wallpaper. It leads nowhere, then everywhere, then back into your own sleeve. The Backrooms did not invent this maze. It only rented the carpet.
