The Crawlspace

The duct is barely wider than your ribs. You drag yourself forward on your stomach, your face inches from the dust-choked sheet metal.

Your shoulders scrape the sides with every movement, creating a loud screech that echoes down the grid. You hear something else sliding through a parallel vent above you—fast, metallic clattering that stops whenever you freeze.

The air is hot, stale, and smells of old grease. You can feel the panic rising in your chest.

DUCT TRANSMISSION

The vent carries scraps of other pages: a laugh from Lanci, a warroom status ping, a fox tapping once from inside the wall. The sound stops when you understand it.
